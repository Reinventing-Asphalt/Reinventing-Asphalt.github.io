Overview

Discarded rubber tyres are flooding the landfills.

In order to help the situation, there are asphalt mixes that use rubber tyres as ingredients.

However, these mixes are expensive and have extremely limited shelf-life.

A new mix is being developed to solve those problems.

The Ingredients

Crumb Rubber (CRMA)

Discarded rubber tyres are recycled from landfills to be ground down into a fine powder. This powder, when added to an asphalt mix (CRMA) will increase its stiffness. However, this mix suffers 2 major disadvantages.

×

Crumb Rubber (CRMA)

  • CRMA requires a high temperature for production. This is expensive and creates noxious fumes.
  • CRMA asphalt mixes have an extremely limited shelf life. If not used, it degrades in under a day.

The Mixing

Mixing of TB asphalt rubber with warm additives is more energy efficient compared to the CRMA mix.

×

The Mixing

  • Due to TB asphalt requiring a far lower working (mixing) temperature, 140 degrees celsius compared to 190 degrees, much less energy is used in the mixing process.
  • Because is this, less oil is burned in the production of TB asphalt and costs and carbon emmisions are reduced.

Construction

Use of TB asphalt for construction is far better for workers' health than using CRMA mix.

×

Construction

  • Due to the high working temperature of CRMA, during construction, the some of the bitumen in CRMA will boil and release harmful fumes.
  • The lower working temperature of TB means the bitumen's chemicals will not reach boiling point and the workers are protected from the fumes.
